## How Does an Online-Compatible TV Work? Understanding the Basics and Benefits
 A TV with internet is a television designed to[connect to the internet](https://www.lifewire.com/connect-smart-tv-to-wifi-4587350) and display content from online sources. You can use an internet TV to watch YouTube videos, check the weather, stream Netflix shows, rent movies on Amazon Prime, or perform any other task available on the internet.

 In most ways, an internet-equipped TV (often called a[smart TV](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-a-smart-tv-4140172) ) delivers the same functionality as a hardware streaming device like a[Roku](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-a-roku-4142216) or[Apple TV](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-apple-tv-1994193) , as well as the usual television channels provided by an antenna or cable/satellite subscription.

## What Services Do You Get on an Internet TV?

 When you are shopping for an internet TV, make sure you find out what features it has. If you are an audiophile, streaming music apps are probably important to you. If you're a gamer, you'll want to check out the video game compatibility. Each manufacturer uses a collection of features that vary from model to model. Popular free and paid features that are available on internet TVs include:

* [Applications that work like mobile apps](https://www.lifewire.com/how-to-add-apps-to-a-smart-tv-4172379)
* [Amazon Video On Demand](https://www.lifewire.com/amazon-free-streaming-for-prime-members-1847379)
* [YouTube](https://www.lifewire.com/youtube-101-3481847)
* [Spotify](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-spotify-4685829)
* [Netflix](https://www.lifewire.com/overview-of-the-netflix-streaming-service-1847831)
* [Hulu](https://www.lifewire.com/what-is-hulu-4783546)
* Live broadcasts
* [Sports](https://www.lifewire.com/best-free-sports-streaming-sites-4169519)
* Video games
* X (formerly Twitter), Facebook and other social networking apps
* News and print publication channels
* [Music services](https://www.lifewire.com/top-places-to-listen-to-free-music-online-1356649) (Napster, Pandora, Slacker)
* Photo services
* Weather

## What You Need

 To[use the internet-enabled functions on a TV](https://www.lifewire.com/internet-enable-your-tv-3276356) , you must connect the television to the internet. In most cases, this can be done wirelessly (which requires a wireless router), but some televisions require a[wired Ethernet connection](https://www.lifewire.com/home-theater-connection-photo-gallery-4064167) . After the TV is connected to your wireless router or directly to your modem by cable, it uses your high-speed broadband internet connection to deliver internet content.

 There is no additional charge for the basic internet functionality on the TV, but some services, such as [Netflix](https://www.lifewire.com/overview-of-the-netflix-streaming-service-1847831) and[Amazon Video](https://www.lifewire.com/amazon-prime-video-4176363) , have subscription charges if you want to use the services. You may need to upgrade your internet data limit with your internet provider if you find yourself streaming a large amount of content.
