##  1\. Fix WhatsApp Crashing on iPhone Problem

 When you run WhatsApp on your iPhone X/8/Plus or iPhone 7/6s/6 but end with crashing problem, you can try the below solutions to fix it on your own before resorting to app support team or asking for help from somebody else. 

![solve WhatsApp Crashing on iPhone issue](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/article-image/whatsapp-crash.jpg) 

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png)   **Close the app**  on iPhone and re-launch it to have a try. 

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png)   **Disable Facebook sync** if you have recently installed Facebook with contact sync enabled to your iPhone Address Book. 

Open Facebook app on iPhone > click "More" icon > select **General** to enter Settings page > Find **"Upload contacts"** and turn green button to grey > tap "Turn Off" on the pop-up window with "Stop continuously uploading your contacts" notification words.

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Close **Auto Backup** in WhatsApp. Auto Backup is used to automatically backup chats to iCloud. But sometimes, it might cause your crashing on iPhone. To turn off Auto Backup, go to Settings and find Chat Backup under Chat category on WhatsApp > tap "Chat Backup" to enter Auto Backup interface > turn off Auto Backup. 

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) If nothing good happens after you've tried all the above methods, please uninstall WhatsApp and re-install it on your iPhone to have a shot. 

## 2\. Fix Couldn't Connect to WhatsApp Service Problem

Sometimes, one problem results from a spate of complicated reasons and more often than not you just can't instantly figure out which one is the chief culprit. In such case, you'd better try more practical solutions to test which one can solve your problem. As for this connection problem, you're suggested to try more with the below solutions.

![WhatsApp connection issue fixed](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/article-image/whatsapp-network.jpg) 

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Go to Settings > Click Cellular > Find WhatsApp and toggle the button to green > pull down to turn on "Wi-Fi Assist".

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Close other [iPhone apps](https://tools.techidaily.com/macxdvd/products/) running on your iPhone background to make sure they doesn't consume too much bandwidth and affect WhatsApp.

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Empty app cache (Settings > Applications > WhatsApp > Clear Cache).

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Forget your Wi-Fi network and reconnect it. Navigate to Settings on iPhone > find Wi-Fi and tap the information button (icon with exclamation mark inside) > tap "Forget This Network" > Return to Wi-Fi selection after a while, choose a Wi-Fi network from the Wi-Fi list and enter password. 

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Exit the proxy or VPN services on iPhone, for WhatsApp isn't designed to be used with proxy or VPN services.

![](https://www.macxdvd.com/mobile/../image-style/kids-dvd-movie/disc02.png) Shut down and reboot your iPhone X/8/Plus or iPhone 7/6s/6/Plus if the mentioned solutions don't work for you.
