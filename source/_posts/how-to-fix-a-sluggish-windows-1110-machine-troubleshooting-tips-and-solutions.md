## Method 1: Disable Fast Startup

```` `` The first quick and easy method to solve the problem is to disable Fast Startup on your Windows 10 device.

 Fast Startup is a default-enabled feature in Windows 10\. It is supposed to reduce startup time by pre-loading some boot information before your PC shuts off. It works similarly as Hibernation. But many users have reported it’s causing issues.

```` ``

```` `` 1) Type **power option**  in the search box at Start, then click **Power & Sleep settings** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/10/img_59e5773042997.png)

```` ``

```` `` 2) Click **Choose what the power button does** .

```` ``

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/2-29.png)

```` ``

```` ``

```` `` 3) Click **Change settings that are currently unavailable** .

```` ``

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/3-21.png)

```` ``

```` ``

```` `` 4) Untick **Turn on fast startup (recommended)** . Then Click **Save Changes** .

```` ``

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/4-18.png)

```` ``

 5) Reboot your Windows 10 device and check to see if it boots quickly.  If you’re still experiencing a slow boot, you can move on to our next method.
