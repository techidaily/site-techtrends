### No.1: Spotify

With a huge music library of over 30 million songs, irresistibly low $0 price tag with ads and $9.99 per month without ads, great features, Spotify music is of course among the best music services. It also provides the best music streaming app for both desktop devices and major mobile devices of iOS, Android and more. In fact, Spotify reported 15 million paid subscribers and more than 60 million users early this year, becoming one of the favorite [online music streaming site](https://tools.techidaily.com/macxdvd/products/)among users.

**Sound Quality:** MP3 96Kbps - stand quality for mobile; 160Kbps for desktop and "high quality" mobile streaming; and 320Kbps for the the high bitrate setting (desktop, iOS and Android).

**Price:** Free with ads/$10 (£10, AU$12) a month for Spotify Premium.

**Mobile devices supported:** Android, iOS, BlackBerry, Windows Phone.

Pros

More than 30 million song catalog

Excellent audio quality 320Kbps

Great social integration with Facebook, Offline listening

Compatible with iPod Touch, iPhone, iPad, Android, Windows and home audio systems.

Cons

Facebook integration comes privacy problems

Lack of variety is a minor complaint

Spotify not working in some cases

### No.2: Pandora

[Pandora](https://tools.techidaily.com/macxdvd/products/) is another best music streaming app with the maximum compatibility. However, it's a little bit different from other recognized names among streaming music services, providing music more like a radio. The streaming services also comes as free and premium flavors, with the premium service will set you back $5/month or $55/year. On this best streaming music service, you can create internet radio stations based on genres, artists, or songs, and thumb up or down the songs you hear. 

**Sound Quality:** 128Kbps for non-subscribers and peaks at 192Kbps for Pandora One users.

**Price:** Unlimited music with advertisements or $54.89/year or $4.99/month with no ads.

**Mobile devices supported:** Android, iOS, BlackBerry, Windows Phone.

Pros

Music recommendations based on the Music Genome Project

Free with ads or cheap premium edition Available on most devices.

Cons

Only has about one million songs

Can't pick songs on-demand

Limited amount of skips

Poor social integration.

That's left to the users. 

**Sound Quality:** 256kbps

**Price:** $9.99 per month or $14.99/month for 6 people.

**Mobile devices supported:** Android, iOS, Mac and PC.

256kps is worse in quality than 320 kps

### No.4: Google Play Music

Google Play Music is the one in this list of best music streaming sites with the largest music resource. Right, with more than 30 million songs to choose from and high sound quality in 320kbps MP3 format, music lovers will have a great time on this best music streaming service. Users can upload up to 50,000 songs to their Google Play Music and use it as a great cloud-based music storage services as well. A 30-day free trial is available, if you want to enjoy unlimited music on this best online music services, you will need to pay $9.99 per month. 

**Sound Quality:** 320 kbps excellent sound quality on desktop and mobile with difference between MP3 and AAC.

**Price:** $10 (£10, AU$10) a month with no free option.

**Mobile devices supported**: Android, iOS

Pros

Large music library of 30+ million songs

Store specific songs on your mobile app and listen while offline

Upload up to 50,000 songs

### No.5: Rdio

Rdio and Spotify are alike in many aspects: both have over 32 million songs, subscription at $9.99 per month and both works on Android, iOS, BlackBerry and Windos Phone. Although Rdio is similar to Spotify in many ways, it’s simplicity, broad mobile device support and cleaner users interface allow Rido to make the list of top music streaming services. Rdio can stream at 64kbps, 96kbps and 192kbps in the settings. If you want to enjoy 320kbps on this best music streaming app, join the Rdio Unlimited subscribers first. 

**Sound Quality:** 192Kbps by default, but premium users can select 320Kbps.

**Price:** Free with ads / $10 (£10, AU$12) a month for all access pass.

**Mobile devices supported**: Android, iOS, BlackBerry, Windows Phone.

Pros

32 million available songs

Glean and smart user interface

Great social integration with social networks

Easy to discover new music

Offline listening

Compatible with iPod Touch, iPhone, iPad, Android, Windows and Blackberry.

Cons

Some network connectivity issues like drop outs, losing the ends of tracks.
