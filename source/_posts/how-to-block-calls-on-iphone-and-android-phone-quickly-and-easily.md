### Method 1: Block trackers with the Content Blocking feature in Firefox

 With the Content Blocking feature, you can create a tracker-free zone when surfing the web with Firefox. To adjust the Content Blocking feature in Firefox:

 1) In Firefox, click**the menu button** on the upper-right corner, then select**Options** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-675.png)

 2) On the left, click**Privacy & Security** . If you never modify the Content Blocking feature, the Firefox should be set to the Standard mode by default. In the Standard mode, Firefox only blocks known trackers in Private Windows.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-676.png)

 3) You can select the Strict mode. Under the Strict mode, Firefox blocks all trackers it detects. Please note that it may cause some site to break.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-677.png)

 4) If you need the ultimate control over the Content Blocking feature, the Custom mode is for you. In the Custom mode, you decide what to block, including trackers, cookies and more.

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-678.png)

### Method 3: Block pop-up windows for specific websites

 You can also block pop-up windows for specific websites in Firefox. Here is how to do it:

1) Open the website you want to block ads in Firefox.

2) Click**the lock icon** next to**the address bar** in Firefox, then click**the gear icon** next to**Permissions** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-743.png)

 3) Make sure you**check the box** next to**Block pop-up windows** .

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/image-746.png)
